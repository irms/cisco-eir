How The Program Works

Cisco EIR@ Fresno is a joint-incubation program which fosters the development of startups in the San Joaquin Valley that are innovating in the Internet of Everything, Big Data and Cloud solutions.

Based in Fresno, Calif. the program is open to startups located within the eight-county area of the San Joaquin Valley (Kern, Tulare, Kings, Fresno, Madera, Merced, Stanislaus or San Joaquin County).

Applications for CiscoEIR@Fresno open in the fourth quarter of 2014. The first cohort of startups will begin their incubation in the first half of 2015. Selected companies will receive mentoring, financial support and other resources to accelerate their product and business development.

 

Program Benefits

  • Access to advisors, mentors, program events and facilities of the Lyles Center, the WET Center and Bitwise Industries
  • A six-month residency at a Bitwise Industries facility in Downtown Fresno
  • Connections with Cisco and its global partner ecosystem and the diverse entrepreneurial community in the San Joaquin Valley
  • Opportunities to collaborate with Cisco engineering and product groups
  • Access and introduction to funding sources (local angel funding groups, New California Ventures LLC, venture capital organizations)

 

Program Requirements

  • Technology domain focus (including: Internet of Everything, Security, Cloud Computing, Big Data and Analytics, Enterprise Mobility, Knowledge Management and Collaboration, Developer Platforms and Tools)
  • Located in the San Joaquin Valley (Bakersfield to Stockton) with a Central Valley commitment
  • Early-stage company with innovative ideas
  • Market viability with a scalable business model
  • Possess a working prototype, if applicable
